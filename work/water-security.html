Water Security

Our Basic Infrastructure focuses on getting people in rural areas stable access to clean water.

BESI

Building Environmentally Sustainable ​Infrastructure

The word “besi” in Tetun means iron, and this program seeks to strengthen communities through the sustainable construction of water resources for those that need it. With this initiative we travel to different communities in order to construct wells, water ways for irrigation and public fountains. From 2011 to 2018 we have ensured 42 communities have clean water, reaching 1243 households and 7345 people.

ALFA

Building restrooms in rural areas and teaching hygienic practices

In Timor we have encountered the problem of many rural communities either not having the proper waste management systems or not understanding proper hygiene practices. That’s why we started the program ALFA. So far we have built 45 bathrooms and have had seminar hygiene seminars in 30 communities.

WASH

Maintaining quality bathrooms in schools and teaching hygiene to students

WASH is similar to our other Water Security initiatives but with a specific focus towards the schools in the communities we serve. With the added effort on teaching students about proper hygiene and menstrual health. Clean water and proper bathrooms are very important for keeping a students comfortable and in a good environment to learn.
