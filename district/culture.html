Culture

Culture runs deep in Timor-Leste and just as deep in our municipality of Manufahi.

Ancestral houses (Uma Lulik)

Dwellings steeped in tradition

These houses follow the same models that of our ancestors and hold special importance in the each Timorese family. Each year members of the family must return to the Uma Lulik to pay respect to the family line and those that came before. On the inside special mementos from the family are kept in an enclosing, giving the homes a sacred importance.

Tais Timor

Timor's oldest weaving tradition

The practice of weaving Tais goes back centuries and is still alive and vibrant today. Each region of Timor-Leste has their own pattern and special color of Tais. Tais are usually worn during traditional ceremonies and given as gifts as a symbol of deference and humility.

Traditional Clothing (Hatais Tais)

Clothing Fit for Kings

The Traditional clothing of a Timorese leader (Liruai) is pictured to the right. The headress resembles a the horns of the Timorese water buffalo, culturally thought of as one of the strongest animals in Timor. The large medallions indicate hierarchical importance and the larger the gold medallion the more power your family line had.
