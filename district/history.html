History

The history of Timor-Leste and our community of Same, Manufahi is a long and winding story, filled with heroes and villains a like. Through it all the people endure and we serve the people.

Dom Boaventura

Leader of the 1912 Resistance

No other leader embodies the spirt of Manufahi like Dom Boaventura and he has many statues that still stand like the one above. His rebellion against the Portuguese colonizers in 1912 was one of the first agressive acts of self-determinism by the people of Manufahi and that fighting spirit still survives today.

Coinage

Local history etched into currency

As you may know Timor-Leste is a country that uses the dollar as its currency but it uses its own distinct coins. In 2012, the Democratic Republic of Timor-Leste allowed states to choose their own images for the 1 face of the dollar coin. Who else could we choose but the man we all refer to affecitonately as "Grandfather" or "Avo, Dom Boaventura.

Flag of Manufahi

We the people

We take great pride in where we come from and this flag is representative of that. On the left side there is a strong triangle protruding out in our country's colors. This represents the mountain of Kablaki which is talllest in Manufahi and third tallest in Timor-Leste. Also next to that is our traditional headress and medallion worn during traditional events.
